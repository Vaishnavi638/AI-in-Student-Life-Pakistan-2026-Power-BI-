# AI-in-Student-Life-Pakistan-2026-Power-BI-

📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes how students in Pakistan are using Artificial Intelligence tools in their academic life and how it impacts their performance, satisfaction, and behavior.
The dashboard is built using Power BI and presents insights from 100 students across different education levels (School, College, University) and major cities including Karachi, Lahore, Multan, Islamabad, and Faisalabad.

🎯 Objectives

- Understand AI tool adoption among students
- Analyze the impact of AI usage on academic performance
- Identify behavioral patterns based on usage, gender, and age
- Compare effectiveness of different AI tools
- Evaluate whether higher usage leads to better academic outcomes

🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Power BI – Dashboard creation & visualization
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Measures & calculated columns
- Data Cleaning & Transformation – Handled missing values and structured dataset

📂 Dataset Details

- Total Records: 100 students
- Features include:
- Gender
- Age Group
- City
- Education Level
- AI Tool Used (ChatGPT, Gemini, Grammarly, Copilot, Notion AI)
- Daily Usage (hours)
- Purpose (Coding, Homework, Learning, Research, Writing)
- Satisfaction Level
- Grade Impact (Improved / No Change / Declined)

KPI Cards:

- % Grade Declined

Visuals:

- AI Tool Usage Distribution (Donut Chart)

🔹 Page 2: Behavioral & Pattern Analysis

Visuals:

📈 Key Insights

- Gemini and Grammarly are the most used AI tools (24% each)
- Only 39% of students experienced grade improvement
- 27% of students saw a decline in grades
- ChatGPT shows the highest effectiveness (13/20 users improved)
- AI usage for Coding has the highest improvement rate (11/20)
- High usage (>3 hrs) does not guarantee better performance
- Low usage (<3 hrs) shows more balanced academic outcomes
- Female students report lower satisfaction compared to males
- Age group 21+ has the highest usage but still shows grade decline cases
- Students with low satisfaction have the highest daily usage (3.3 hrs/day)
- School students use AI more than College and University students
- AI usage for Writing shows the lowest improvement rate

💡 Key Learnings

- More AI usage does not always lead to better academic results
- The effectiveness of AI depends heavily on purpose of use
- Students using AI for productive tasks like coding benefit more
- Behavioral factors (gender, satisfaction, age) influence outcomes
